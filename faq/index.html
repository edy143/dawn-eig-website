Frequently Asked Questions

What can I expect from therapy?

Therapy is a collaborative process that can open new possibilities, deepen self-awareness, and shift the way you experience your relationships. My goal is to help you move toward a more authentic way of living -- one that feels grounded, meaningful, and fulfilling.

What is the difference between psychotherapy and psychoanalysis?

Psychotherapy tends to focus on identifying patterns in thoughts and behaviors that are more immediately accessible and conscious, often involving practical strategies to address symptoms. A psychoanalytic approach looks at symptoms as expressions of underlying emotional conflicts, often outside of awareness, and seeks to understand not only what you are experiencing, but why. The primary practical distinction is frequency: psychoanalysis typically involves meeting more often, which allows for greater continuity and depth.

How often do we meet?

The choice of frequency depends on what feels manageable and appropriate for you. Once-weekly psychotherapy can be highly effective and transformative. When it is possible to meet more often, the added consistency and intensity can strengthen the therapeutic relationship and support a more in-depth exploration.

Do you offer online sessions?

I am available to work by Zoom, but I find that working together in person is far preferable. There is no substitute for being in the presence of another. I am happy to discuss what makes the most sense for your situation.

What are your fees?

My standard fee is discussed during the initial consultation. For patients who qualify, I have a sliding fee scale, which is to say that I reserve a percentage of my practice for people who need lower fees.

Do you take insurance?

I'm not in network with any insurance companies. If you have insurance and have out of network benefits, you can be reimbursed by your insurance company for sessions with me. I'll provide you with a monthly superbill. Check with your insurer to see if you have out of network benefits.

How do I get started?

Beginning therapy can feel like an important and sometimes uncertain step, and finding the right fit matters. I offer a complimentary 15-minute phone consultation as an opportunity for us to connect, talk about what you're looking for, and see whether working together feels like a good match. You can reach me by phone at (917) 494-6886 or by email at dawneiglp@gmail.com.
